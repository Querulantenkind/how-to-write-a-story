## **Act**

A major division of a story that changes the overall situation and resets goals/stakes.

## **Beat**

A small unit of story change, often a moment of decision, reaction, or new information.

## **Turning Point**

A moment that shifts the direction of the story or scene so it canâ€™t return to the previous status quo.

## **Inciting Incident**

The event that disrupts the protagonistâ€™s normal world and introduces the central problem.

## **Rising Action**

The sequence of escalating complications that increase tension and narrow options.

## **Climax**

The peak confrontation where the central conflict is decided.

## **Resolution (Denouement)**

The aftermath that shows consequences and the new normal.

## **Scene**

A unit of story that contains conflict or pressure and produces change.

## **Sequel**

A reflective unit following a scene where characters process what happened, make decisions, and set new goals.

## **Stakes**

What the character stands to gain or lose in a scene or story.

## **Escalation**

The process of making conflict more intense: higher costs, fewer options, stronger opposition.

## **Cause and Effect**

A chain where events happen because of choices and consequences (instead of coincidence).
