## âœ‚ï¸ **Minimalism: The Art of Less**

### **Philosophy**

> "What's left unsaid is as important as what's said."

Minimalism trusts the reader. It provides concrete details and lets readers draw their own conclusions. It believes that restraint creates power.

---

### **Characteristics of Minimalist Prose**

**1. Economy of Language**
Every word must earn its place.

**Example:**
> "He left. She stayed. The door closed."

---

**2. Concrete Specifics**
Minimalism favors the particular over the general.

**Not:**
> "She felt sad."

**Instead:**
> "She stared at his empty chair."

---

**3. Short Sentences and Paragraphs**
Brief, declarative statements.

**Example:**
> "The rain stopped. We got out of the car. The street was empty. All the houses were dark."

---

**4. Emotional Restraint**
Feelings shown through action and detail, never named.

**Example:**
> "She folded his shirts. She placed them in the box. She closed the lid. She taped it shut. On the box, she wrote his name."

**What's happening:** Grief, loss, finalityâ€”all conveyed without emotional adjectives.

---

**5. Subtext and Implication**
Meaning lives beneath the surface.

**Example (Hemingway's "Hills Like White Elephants"):**
> "The hills across the valley of the Ebro were long and white."

**On surface:** Description of landscape  
**Beneath:** Metaphor for pregnancy, abortion, their entire relationship

### **Mistake 1: Confusing Minimal with Boring**

**Bad minimal:**
> "She was sad. She cried. She went home."

**Good minimal:**
> "She stared at the wall. Her coffee got cold. Outside, cars passed. She didn't move."

**Difference:** Good minimalism uses concrete detail and trusts subtext.

---

### **Mistake 2: Confusing Maximal with Purple**

**Bad maximal (purple prose):**
> "Her eyes were like pools of cerulean beauty, sparkling with the luminescent quality of a thousand stars twinkling in the vast expanse of the infinite cosmos."

**Good maximal:**
> "Her eyes held the particular blue of October skiesâ€”that heartbreaking clarity that comes just before winter, when the air turns sharp and everything feels both more vivid and more fragile."

**Difference:** Good maximalism serves the story; purple prose serves the ego.
