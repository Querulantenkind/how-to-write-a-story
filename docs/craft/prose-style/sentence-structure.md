## ðŸŽ¨ **Sentence Structure Techniques**

### **1. Parallelism**

Repeating grammatical structures for emphasis and rhythm.

**Example:**
> "She was tired of pretending, tired of smiling, tired of being the person everyone needed her to be."

> "He came. He saw. He left."

**Effect:** Creates rhythm, builds intensity, emphasizes connections.

---

### **2. Inversion**

Reversing normal word order for emphasis.

**Normal:**
> "A dark figure stood in the doorway."

**Inverted:**
> "In the doorway stood a dark figure."

**Effect:** Draws attention, creates suspense, varies rhythm.

---

### **3. Interruption**

Using dashes or parentheses to break sentence flow.

**Example:**
> "She reached for the handleâ€”her fingers tremblingâ€”and pulled."

> "The letter (if you could call it that) contained only three words."

**Effect:** Adds information, mimics thought patterns, creates intimacy.

### **4. Fragment**

Intentionally incomplete sentence for effect.

**Example:**
> "Should she tell him? Maybe. Maybe not. Probably never."

> "The smell of smoke. Burning. Close."

**Effect:** Mimics thought, creates urgency, emphasizes feeling over grammar.

---

### **5. Cumulative (Loose) Sentence**

Main clause first, then additions.

**Example:**
> "She walked into the room, her heart pounding, her palms sweating, every instinct screaming at her to run."

**Effect:** Natural, easy to follow, builds detail.

---

### **6. Periodic Sentence**

Building suspense by delaying the main clause until the end.

**Example:**
> "Through the dense fog, past the cemetery gates, down the muddy path where no one had walked in decades, she went."

**Effect:** Creates suspense, demands attention, literary quality.

### **1. All the Same Length**

**Problem:**
