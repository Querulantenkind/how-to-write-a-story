## âš ï¸ **Common Mistakes**

### **Mistake 1: Literary Snobbery**

**Problem:** Dismissing commercial fiction as "lesser."

**Truth:** Writing a gripping, well-plotted commercial novel is incredibly difficult. Different skills, same dedication.

---

### **Mistake 2: Commercial Anti-Intellectualism**

**Problem:** Dismissing literary fiction as "pretentious" or "boring."

**Truth:** The best literary fiction enlarges our understanding of human experience. It's valuable work.

---

### **Mistake 3: Writing Literary While Wanting Commercial Success**

**Problem:** Writing experimental, slow, ambiguous fiction and wondering why it doesn't sell like a thriller.

**Solution:** Understand your market. Know what you're writing and who it's for.

---

### **Mistake 4: Writing Commercial While Wanting Literary Respect**

**Problem:** Writing genre fiction and feeling defensive about its "legitimacy."

**Solution:** Own it. Commercial fiction is legitimate. It doesn't need literary validation.

---

### **Mistake 5: Mimicking Without Understanding**

**Problem:** Adding "literary" elements without purpose, or commercial elements that feel forced.

**Solution:** Every choice should serve your story, not satisfy a category checklist.
