## ğŸ’¡ **Quick Tips**

### **Finding Your Narrator's Voice:**

**Ask these questions:**
1. What's their **age, background, education** level?
2. What's their **emotional state** during the story?
3. What's their **personality** type? (Optimist? Cynic? Naive? Jaded?)
4. What **speech patterns** do they have?
5. What are their **obsessions** or recurring thoughts?
6. How do they **cope with stress** or difficulty?

**Exercise:**
Write the same scene from three different first-person narrators. Notice how the facts stay the same but the story changes completely.

### **Handling Time and Retrospection:**

**Subtle future references:**
> I should have turned around right then. But hindsight is useless when you're twenty-two and stupid.

> Now, fifteen years later, I can admit I was jealous. But that morning, I told myself I was being protective.

**Present tense (no retrospection):**
> I should turn around. But I'm twenty-two, and I know everything, so I keep walking.
