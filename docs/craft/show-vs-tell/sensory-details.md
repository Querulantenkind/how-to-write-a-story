## âŒ **Common Mistakes**

### **1. Sensory Overload**

**Too Much:**
> "The crimson velvet curtains framed the azure sky while golden sunlight streamed through the crystal window, illuminating the mahogany desk topped with a jade plant whose emerald leaves rustled in the breeze that carried the scent of jasmine..."

**Better:**
> "Sunlight caught the dust motes drifting past the velvet curtains. Somewhere, jasmine bloomed."

### **2. Generic Descriptions**

**Boring:**
> "The food smelled good."

**Better:**
> "Garlic and rosemary, butter sizzling."

### **3. Using Only Sight**

**One-Dimensional:**
> "The garden had roses, tulips, and a fountain in the center."

**Multi-Sensory:**
> "Roses climbed the trellis, their perfume thick in the humid air. Water splashed in the fountain, a constant burbling that almost masked the buzz of bees."

### **4. Describing Everything**

**Info-Dump:**
> "The room had four walls painted beige, with three windows on the north wall, two doors on opposite sides, a ceiling fan with five blades..."

**Selective:**
> "The ceiling fan turned lazily, doing nothing against the heat."

## ğŸ¯ **Practice Exercises**

### **Exercise 1: Single-Sense Focus**

Take a scene and rewrite it five times, focusing on a different sense each time:
1. Version 1: Sight only
2. Version 2: Sound only
3. Version 3: Smell only
4. Version 4: Taste only
5. Version 5: Touch only

Then combine the best details from each.

### **Exercise 2: The Sense You Ignore**

Identify which sense you use least in your writing. Write 500 words focusing primarily on that sense.

### **Exercise 3: Character-Specific Senses**

Write the same scene from three different character POVs. Each character should notice different sensory details based on their background:
- A chef
- A musician
- A detective

### **Exercise 4: Sensory Memory**

List ten scents/sounds/textures from your childhood. Write a scene where one triggers a memory.

### **Exercise 5: Synesthesia**

Describe one sense using another sense:
- "The music tasted like honey"
- "The silence felt rough"
- "The color shrieked"
