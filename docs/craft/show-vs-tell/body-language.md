## ğŸ“ **Spatial Behavior & Proximity**

### **PROXEMICS (Personal Space)**

**Intimate Distance (0-18 inches):**
- Lovers, very close friends, family
- Invasion = threat or attraction

**Personal Distance (18 inches - 4 feet):**
- Friends, comfortable conversation
- Most social interaction

- Professional, casual acquaintances
- Maintains boundaries

**Public Distance (12+ feet):**
- Formal, public speaking
- Safety buffer

## âš–ï¸ **Using Body Language Effectively**

### **âœ… Best Practices:**

**1. Be Specific**
- Not "She gestured"
- But "She waved her hand dismissively"

**2. Make It Do Double Duty**
- Reveal emotion AND character AND move plot

**3. Don't Overuse**
- Not every line of dialogue needs a gesture
- Trust the dialogue sometimes

**4. Vary Your Vocabulary**
- Characters don't always "shrug" or "nod"
- Find fresh physical beats

**5. Consider Context**
- Cultural differences in body language
- Professional vs. personal settings
- Character's normal baseline

**6. Use Contradiction**
- Words say one thing, body says another
- Creates subtext and tension

**7. Show Change**
- Character growth = body language shift
- Nervous character becomes confident through posture change

## âŒ **Common Mistakes**

### **1. ClichÃ© Gestures**

**Overused:**
- Shrugging
- Nodding
- Crossing arms
- Raising eyebrows

**Solution:** Find specific, fresh alternatives.

### **2. Too Much Description**

**Problem:**
> "He raised his left hand to his face, touched his nose with his index finger, then scratched behind his ear while shifting his weight from his left foot to his right."

**Solution:**
> "He touched his noseâ€”lying, definitely lying."

### **3. Impossible Actions**

**Problem:**
> "She shrugged apologetically while waving dismissively and nodding in agreement."

*Can't do all that at once.*

### **4. Every Line Gets a Beat**

**Problem:**
> "I'm fine," she said, crossing her arms.
> "Are you sure?" he asked, raising an eyebrow.
> "Yes," she said, looking away.
> "Okay," he said, nodding.

**Solution:** Not every line needs a gesture. Let dialogue breathe.

### **5. Telling After Showing**

**Problem:**
> "His fists clenched. He was angry."

**Solution:**
> "His fists clenched."

*You showed it. Don't also tell it.*
