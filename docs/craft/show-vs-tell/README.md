### **Exercise 2: The Showing Audit**

Take a page of your manuscript:
- Highlight all instances of telling
- Identify which need to become showing
- Leave efficient telling alone

### **Exercise 3: Five Senses Scene**

Write a scene engaging at least three of the five senses. Focus on specific, evocative details.

### **Exercise 4: Emotion Without Naming**

Write a scene where a character experiences a strong emotion (grief, rage, joy). Show it without ever naming the emotion.

### **Exercise 5: The Body Language Dictionary**

Observe people (or watch TV with sound off). List 20 distinct gestures/expressions and what emotions they convey.

## ğŸ’­ **Philosophy of Show vs. Tell**

### **It's Not a Ruleâ€”It's a Tool**

The "rule" of "show, don't tell" is misunderstood. The truth:

> **Both showing and telling are essential tools. Master writers know when to use each.**

### **Showing:**
- Creates experience
- Immersive and vivid
- Slower, detailed
- Engages readers actively
- More sophisticated

### **Telling:**
- Provides information
- Efficient and clear
- Faster, summarized
- Direct communication
- Equally valid when used well

**The art** is knowing which technique serves each moment best.
