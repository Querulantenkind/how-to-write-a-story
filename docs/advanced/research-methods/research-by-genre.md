## üìñ **Overview**

Different genres have different research requirements. A historical novel demands exhaustive period research; a contemporary romance needs emotional authenticity over technical details; a hard science fiction story requires scientific rigor.

This guide breaks down research needs by genre.

## üèõÔ∏è **Historical Fiction**

### **Research Intensity:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê (Highest)

### **What to Research:**

#### **Essential (Before Writing):**

**Time Period Basics:**
- Major historical events
- Social structures and class systems
- Political climate
- Technology available
- Communication methods
- Transportation
- Money and economy

**Daily Life:**
- What people ate (by class)
- Clothing (practical and formal)
- Housing and furniture
- Work and occupations
- Education
- Recreation and entertainment
- Hygiene and health practices

**Cultural Context:**
- Social norms and taboos
- Gender roles and expectations
- Religious practices
- Racial and ethnic dynamics
- Language and speech patterns
- Manners and etiquette

#### **Important (During Writing):**

**Specific Locations:**
- Geography and landmarks
- Architecture and interiors
- Street names and layouts
- Local industries
- Climate and seasons

**Historical Events:**
- Exact dates and timelines
- Who was involved
- Contemporary reactions
- Long-term consequences

**Material Culture:**
- Specific objects and their uses
- Fashion details
- Tools and equipment
- Books, art, music of the period

#### **Details (Revision):**

**Language:**
- Period-appropriate vocabulary
- Slang and colloquialisms
- What words didn't exist yet
- Regional dialects

**Verification:**
- Did this place exist then?
- Was this technology available?
- Did these people overlap?
- Is this timeline plausible?

---

### **Common Historical Fiction Mistakes:**

‚ùå **Anachronisms:**
- Using modern expressions
- Including technology that didn't exist
- Imposing modern values on historical characters

‚ùå **Info-dumping:**
- Lecturing about history instead of showing it
- Characters explaining obvious-to-them facts

‚ùå **Romanticizing the past:**
- Ignoring period bigotry and prejudice
- Making everyone too clean and comfortable
- Skipping over harsh realities

---

### **Historical Research Strategy:**

**Phase 1: Immerse**
- Read 3-5 history books about period
- Watch period documentaries
- Read novels written IN that period (not about it)

**Phase 2: Specifics**
- Consult archives for primary sources
- Study period newspapers
- Read letters and diaries
- Look at photographs or artwork

**Phase 3: Expert Check**
- Consult historians
- Join period-specific online communities
- Hire sensitivity readers for cultural elements

---

### **Key Resources:**
- Academic history books
- Primary source archives
- Period newspapers and magazines
- Museum collections
- Historical societies
- *The Writer's Guide to Everyday Life* series

## üîç **Mystery & Thriller**

### **Research Intensity:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê (High)

### **What to Research:**

#### **Essential:**

**Law Enforcement:**
- Police procedures and protocols
- Jurisdictional boundaries
- Chain of evidence
- Miranda rights (if US)
- Detective ranks and roles
- Real investigation timelines
- What police can/can't legally do

**Forensics:**
- Crime scene processing
- Evidence collection
- Laboratory analysis timelines
- What tests actually reveal
- Limitations of forensic science
- Legal admissibility

**Legal System:**
- Arrest to trial process
- Rights of accused
- How courts actually work
- Lawyer roles and limitations
- Plea bargains
- Search warrants

**Weapons (if applicable):**
- How specific weapons work
- Realistic injury patterns
- Ballistics
- Gun laws and licensing

#### **Important:**

**Medical Details:**
- Causes and time of death
- Autopsy procedures
- Injury descriptions
- Drug effects and detection

**Technical Elements:**
- Hacking (realistic capabilities)
- Surveillance laws
- Phone and internet tracking
- Security systems

---

### **Common Mystery/Thriller Mistakes:**

‚ùå **CSI Effect:**
- Forensics too fast
- Technology too magical
- Everything trackable instantly

‚ùå **Unrealistic Police:**
- Lone detective doing everything
- Ignoring jurisdictional limits
- Breaking rules without consequences

‚ùå **Legal Errors:**
- Courtroom dramatics that aren't allowed
- Misunderstanding burden of proof
- Confusing civil and criminal procedure

---

### **Mystery Research Strategy:**

**Must Interview:**
- Active or retired law enforcement
- Forensic technicians
- Criminal defense attorneys
- Medical examiners

**Must Read:**
- Police procedural guides
- Forensic science textbooks
- True crime accounts
- Legal case studies

**Must Verify:**
- Jurisdiction-specific laws
- Local police procedures
- Timeline plausibility
- Technical capabilities

---

### **Key Resources:**
- *Police Procedure & Investigation* by Lee Lofland
- *Forensics: A Guide for Writers* by D.P. Lyle
- Local police departments (many offer ride-alongs)
- Legal blogs and forums
- Medical examiner memoirs

## üíñ **Romance**

### **Research Intensity:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê to ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê (Varies by subgenre)

### **What to Research:**

#### **Contemporary Romance:**

**Emotional Authenticity:**
- Relationship dynamics
- Communication patterns
- Attachment styles
- Healthy vs. unhealthy relationships
- Consent and boundaries

**Character Professions:**
- Day-to-day job realities
- Industry jargon
- Work schedule and demands
- Professional conflicts and stress

**Specific Settings:**
- Location details if story is setting-specific
- Cultural dating norms
- Local hangouts and activities

#### **Historical Romance:**

**All Historical Fiction Research PLUS:**
- Courtship customs
- Marriage laws and practices
- Gender roles and restrictions
- Sexual knowledge and attitudes
- Contraception availability
- Scandal and reputation

#### **Paranormal/Fantasy Romance:**

**World-building Research:**
- Mythology and folklore
- Supernatural powers and limitations
- How magic affects relationships

---

### **Common Romance Mistakes:**

‚ùå **Outdated Relationship Dynamics:**
- Pushing past "no"
- Glorifying possessive behavior
- Unhealthy power imbalances presented as romantic

‚ùå **Ignoring Practical Realities:**
- No discussion of contraception/STIs
- Unrealistic job schedules
- Forgetting basic needs (food, sleep, bathroom)

‚ùå **Cultural Insensitivity:**
- Stereotyping based on ethnicity
- Misrepresenting LGBTQ+ relationships
- Appropriating cultures

---

### **Romance Research Strategy:**

**Read Widely:**
- Romance novels in your subgenre
- Relationship psychology books
- Memoirs about relationships

**Understand Consent:**
- Enthusiastic consent
- Communication in relationships
- Trauma-informed approaches

**Know Your Audience:**
- Genre conventions and reader expectations
- Heat level appropriateness
- Current sensitivity around tropes

---

### **Key Resources:**
- *The Five Love Languages* by Gary Chapman
- *Attached* by Amir Levine (attachment theory)
- Romance Writers of America resources
- Relationship psychology research
- Sensitivity readers for diverse characters

## üöÄ **Science Fiction**

### **Research Intensity:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê (for hard SF) or ‚≠ê‚≠ê (for soft SF)

### **What to Research:**

#### **Hard Science Fiction:**

**Core Science:**
- Current scientific understanding
- Theoretical physics
- Space travel mechanics
- Biological sciences
- Computer science and AI
- Engineering principles

**Speculative Extension:**
- What's theoretically possible?
- Current cutting-edge research
- Scientific consensus vs. speculation
- Limitations and constraints

**Technical Realities:**
- Energy requirements
- Timeline plausibility
- Resource needs
- Practical problems

#### **Soft Science Fiction:**

**Social Sciences:**
- Anthropology
- Sociology
- Psychology
- Political systems
- Economics
- Cultural evolution

**Setting Details:**
- Future technology implications
- How society might adapt
- Environmental scenarios
- Social structures

---

### **Common Science Fiction Mistakes:**

‚ùå **Impossible Physics:**
- Ignoring speed of light
- Violating conservation of energy
- Handwaving major scientific principles

‚ùå **Inconsistent Rules:**
- Technology capabilities changing arbitrarily
- Forgetting established limitations
- Mixing incompatible scientific theories

‚ùå **Outdated Science:**
- Basing story on disproven theories
- Ignoring current scientific knowledge

---

### **Science Fiction Research Strategy:**

**Stay Current:**
- Read scientific journals (Science, Nature)
- Follow science news sites
- Listen to science podcasts

**Understand Principles:**
- Take online courses (Khan Academy, MIT OpenCourseWare)
- Read popular science books
- Watch educational video channels

**Consult Experts:**
- University professors
- Working scientists
- Science fiction authors with technical backgrounds
- Science advisors

---

### **Key Resources:**
- *The Science of Science Fiction Writing* by James Gunn
- Scientific American
- ArXiv.org (preprint repository)
- NASA and ESA websites
- Science fiction author forums (for how much accuracy genre expects)

## üêâ **Fantasy**

### **Research Intensity:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê to ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê (Depends on inspiration)

### **What to Research:**

#### **Historical/Cultural Inspiration:**

**If Based on Real Cultures:**
- Mythology and folklore
- Historical events and periods
- Cultural practices and beliefs
- Social structures
- Material culture (clothing, architecture, food)

**Important:** Research DEEPLY if basing on real cultures‚Äîavoid stereotypes and appropriation.

#### **Practical Details:**

**Pre-industrial Technology:**
- Medieval/ancient daily life
- Weapon and armor realities
- Travel times and methods
- Communication methods
- Food production and preservation
- Medicine and healing

**Military and Combat:**
- Historical warfare tactics
- Realistic weapon use
- Armor functionality
- Castle and fortification design
- Logistics of armies

**Economics:**
- How trade worked historically
- Currency and barter systems
- Resource distribution
- Class and wealth

#### **Natural World:**

**Geography and Climate:**
- How terrain affects travel
- Climate impacts on culture
- Natural resources distribution

**Ecology:**
- Food chains and ecosystems
- Animal behavior
- Plant properties

---

### **Common Fantasy Mistakes:**

‚ùå **Unrealistic Medieval Settings:**
- Everyone is filthy and miserable (not quite accurate)
- Ignoring actual medieval diversity
- Assuming universal feudalism

‚ùå **Inconsistent Magic:**
- No rules or limitations
- Powers appear conveniently
- No societal impact of magic

‚ùå **Cultural Appropriation:**
- Surface-level use of real cultures
- Stereotyping and exoticizing
- Taking without understanding

---

### **Fantasy Research Strategy:**

**Build Solid Foundation:**
- Research historical periods for realistic base
- Study actual mythology and folklore
- Understand pre-modern technology

**Develop Consistent Rules:**
- Research physics for magic system logic
- Study economics for realistic societies
- Understand ecology for believable worlds

**Respect Real Cultures:**
- Deep research if drawing inspiration
- Consult people from those cultures
- Hire sensitivity readers
- Consider whether you're the right person to tell this story

---

### **Key Resources:**
- *The Medieval Handbook* series
- Historical arms and armor museums
- Anthropology and folklore texts
- *The Writer's Guide to Fantasy* literature
- World-building guides (*The Art of World Building* by Randy Ellefson)

### **Common Contemporary Fiction Mistakes:**

‚ùå **Dated References:**
- Technology that's already obsolete
- Outdated social media platforms
- Past cultural moments

‚ùå **Generic Settings:**
- Could-be-anywhere descriptions
- Missing local flavor
- Ignoring regional differences

‚ùå **Stereotyping:**

- Relying on tropes

---

### **Contemporary Research Strategy:**

**Live It:**
- Visit settings if possible
- Experience activities characters do
- Talk to people in relevant professions

**Stay Current:**
- Read news and cultural commentary
- Follow social media discourse
- Understand generational differences

**Dive Deep:**
- Interview people with relevant experience
- Read memoirs and personal essays
- Join relevant communities (online or in-person)

## üëª **Horror**

### **Research Intensity:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê (Moderate to High)

### **What to Research:**

#### **Psychological Elements:**
- Fear psychology
- Trauma responses
- Mental illness (accurately portrayed)
- Isolation effects
- Stress reactions

#### **Practical Details:**
- How bodies decompose
- What injuries actually look like
- Medical accuracy in gore
- Realistic survival scenarios

#### **Cultural/Supernatural:**
- Folklore and mythology
- Cultural beliefs about death
- Historical horror events
- Paranormal theories

#### **Setting Atmosphere:**
- Abandoned places (safely researched)
- Isolation locations
- Historical tragedies
- Urban legends (local)

---

### **Common Horror Mistakes:**

‚ùå **Inaccurate Mental Illness:**
- Conflating mental illness with violence
- Stigmatizing representations
- Using outdated terms

‚ùå **Unrealistic Injuries:**
- Characters functioning with impossible wounds
- Incorrect recovery times
- Wrong injury descriptions

‚ùå **Cultural Insensitivity:**
- Appropriating cultural beliefs
- Stereotyping folklore
- Missing spiritual significance

---

### **Horror Research Strategy:**

**Understand Fear:**
- Psychology of horror
- What actually frightens people
- Tension vs. gore

**Research Respectfully:**
- Handle real tragedies with care
- Research cultural elements deeply
- Represent mental illness accurately

**Physical Accuracy:**
- Medical details of injuries
- Realistic survival scenarios
- Actual capabilities of human body

## üîó **Cross-Genre Considerations**

Many stories blend genres‚Äîhistorical fantasy, science fiction mystery, paranormal romance. In these cases:

1. **Identify primary genre:** What drives the story?
2. **Research both sets of requirements**
3. **Prioritize overlap areas:** Where genres intersect
4. **Balance expectations:** Satisfy both genre conventions
