## ðŸ“š **Techniques & Approaches**

### **1. Interior Monologue**

**Definition:**
Character's thoughts presented directly, but with grammatical structure

**Characteristics:**
- First or third person
- Complete thoughts
- Logical connections
- Readable, clear

**Example:**
> She couldn't believe he'd said that. What had he been thinking? Did he actually believe she would just forgive him, after everything? After the lies, the secrets, the betrayal that everyone knew about except her?

**When to Use:**
- Accessible introduction to interiority
- Extended reflection
- Decision-making processes
- Clear character analysis

---

### **2. Free Indirect Discourse**

**Definition:**
Third-person narration that blends with character's thoughts/perspective

**Characteristics:**
- Third person but character's voice
- No quotation marks or "she thought"
- Seamless blending
- Maintains third-person pronouns

**Example:**
> She walked to the window. The rain again. Why was it always raining in this godforsaken town? She'd had enough. Tomorrow she would leave. Tomorrow, definitely tomorrow.

**Note the blend:**
- Objective: "She walked to the window"
- Character thought: "Why was it always raining..."
- No attribution needed

**When to Use:**
- Psychological third person
- Intimacy without first person
- Moving between interiority and action
- Most contemporary literary fiction

---

### **3. Direct Stream**

Unmediated flow of thought, minimal punctuation, associative

**Definition:**
