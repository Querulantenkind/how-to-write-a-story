### **1. Concrete Poetry Techniques in Prose**

**Definition:**
Text shaped to reflect content

**Examples:**

**Shape Poems:**
```
        falling
      falling
    falling
```

- Key moments of narrative
- Metaphorical representation
- Experimental emphasis

**Literary Examples:**
- *House of Leaves* (falling text, maze-like layouts)

- **Descent/ascent:** Text moving down or up page
- **Scatter:** Words spread across space
- **Density:** Tight vs. loose text

### **4. Page Layout Experiments**

**Definition:**
Non-standard arrangement of text on page

**Techniques:**

**Multi-Column:**
```
Column 1:        Column 2:        Column 3:
Sarah's POV      John's POV       Narrator
Simultaneous action in columns
```

**Margin Notes:**
Main text with commentary in margins
> Primary narrative center
> Footnotes, thoughts, corrections in margins
> Create dialogue between text and margins

**Upside Down:**
Text inverted, reader must rotate
> Forces physical engagement
> Represents disorientation
> Challenges reading comfort

**Sideways:**
Text runs horizontally on vertical page
> Reading requires turning book
> Represents altered perspective

**Overlapping:**
Text layered over other text
> Simultaneous voices
> Confusion or chaos
> Palimpsest effect

**Spiral/Circular:**
Text in non-linear paths
> Following text requires navigation
> Represents cycles, labyrinths
> Reader traces path

**When to Use:**
- Multiple simultaneous narratives
- Chaos or confusion themes
- Labyrinths or mazes
- Self-reflexive narratives
- Disorientation

**Examples:**
- *House of Leaves* by Mark Z. Danielewski (all techniques)
- *Only Revolutions* by Mark Z. Danielewski (upside-down dual narrative)
- *The Unfortunates* by B.S. Johnson (loose pages)

### **2. Production Considerations**

**Print:**
- Layout control possible
- Font choices matter
- Printing costs (color, special layouts)
- Binding affects reading (House of Leaves)
- Physical book experience

**Digital:**
- Device variations (fonts, sizing)
- Less layout control
- Different fonts may not transfer
- Interactive possibilities
- Accessibility concerns

**Hybrid:**
- Print for visual experiments
- Digital for interactivity
- Both formats serve different purposes

**Plan For:**
Your publication medium's constraints and possibilities

- Digital accessibility standards

**Solutions:**
- Alternative text descriptions
- Meaningful content accessible other ways
- Balance experiment with clarity
- Consider diverse readers

**Not Excuses:**
