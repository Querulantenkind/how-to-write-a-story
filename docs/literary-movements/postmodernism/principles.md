**Postmodernism** (roughly 1945-2000s) is a movement that **questions the very nature of truth, reality, and storytelling**. Where modernists tried to capture fragmented reality, postmodernists question whether any single "reality" exists at all.

**Core Stance:** All narratives are constructs; let's acknowledge and play with that.

## âœ¨ **Core Principles**

### **1. Rejection of Grand Narratives**

No single story explains everything.

**"Metanarratives" Rejected:**
- Progress is inevitable
- Science solves all problems
- Reason leads to enlightenment
- History has direction/meaning
- One true religion/philosophy

**Postmodern Alternative:**
- Multiple, competing stories
- Local, particular truths
- Irreconcilable perspectives
- Fragmented understanding

---

### **2. Questioning Reality and Truth**

Reality is constructed, not found.

**Key Ideas:**
- **Simulacra** â€” Copies without originals
- **Hyperreality** â€” Simulations more "real" than reality
- **Social construction** â€” "Reality" is agreed-upon fiction
- **Language creates reality** â€” Not just describes it
- **No objective truth** â€” Only perspectives

**In Literature:**
- Unreliable narrators (even more than modernism)
- Contradictory accounts
- Reality/fiction boundaries blurred
- Meta-fictional awareness

---

### **3. Irony and Playfulness**

Self-aware, knowing humor.

**Postmodern Irony:**
- Distance from emotion
- Awareness of artifice
- Pop culture references
- Genre mixing
- Pastiche and parody
- Nothing is sacred

**Tone:**
Often characterized by **detachment**, **cynicism**, and **playfulness** simultaneously.

---

### **4. Intertextuality**

Everything references everything else.

**Postmodern Intertextuality:**
- Texts refer to other texts
- No "original" work
- Meaning comes from web of references
- High/low culture equally valid
- Pastiche (imitation) central

**In Practice:**
Dense with allusions, parodies, homages, and quotations from other works.

---

### **5. Fragmentation and Pastiche**

Mix incompatible elements.

**Techniques:**
- Genre blending
- Style shifting
- Collage/montage
- Juxtaposition of high and low
- Contradictory elements coexisting

**Purpose:**
Reflects cultural fragmentation; challenges unity of meaning.

---

### **6. Anti-Authority**

Question all hierarchies and power structures.

**Postmodern Stance:**
- No authoritative interpretation
- Author's intention irrelevant ("death of the author")
- Reader creates meaning
- Challenge canonical literature
- Marginalized voices elevated
