### **1. Emphasis on Emotion and Intuition**

**Definition:** Feelings and instincts valued over rational thought.

**In Literature:**
- Characters act from passion, not logic
- Emotional reactions described in detail
- Intuition proves correct over calculation
- Inner feelings as plot drivers

**Example Techniques:**

- Character-driven rather than plot-driven
- Gut feelings as wisdom
- Emotional arcs central to story

### **3. Idealization of the Past**

**Definition:** Looking backward for inspiration and values.

**Common Elements:**

- Chivalric codes
- Folk traditions

- Exotic and different
- Not yet "corrupted" by modernity

- Fantasy medieval settings
- Historical romance
- Nostalgia for pre-digital era

## ğŸ“š **Literary Characteristics**

### **Language and Style**

**Elevated Diction:**
- Poetic, musical prose
- Rich vocabulary
- Metaphorical language
- Emotional expressiveness
- Sometimes ornate or "purple"

**Example:**
```
"The moonlight spilled across the heaving ocean like 
liquid silver, each wave a symphony of sorrow that 
echoed the tempest raging within his tormented soul."
```

### **Narrative Techniques**

**Point of View:**
- Often first-person for intimacy
- Deep subjectivity
- Confessional tone
- Unreliable narration (emotion clouds judgment)

**Structure:**
- Frame narratives (story within story)
- Epistolary (letters, diaries)
- Non-linear (following emotional logic)
- Quest or journey structure

**Pacing:**
- Lingering on emotional moments
- Detailed description of settings
- Interior monologue
- Less concerned with plot efficiency

- Simplicity vs. complexity
- Authentic vs. artificial

- Spiritual experience
- Moments of insight
- Connection to something greater
- Beauty as path to truth
