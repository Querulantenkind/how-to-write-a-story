### **Work Backward**

Know the solution first.

**Planning Process:**
1. **Decide who, why, how** â€” The truth
2. **Plant clues** â€” What detective finds
3. **Create red herrings** â€” False leads
4. **Timeline** â€” When things happened
5. **Alibis and motives** â€” For all suspects
6. **Discovery order** â€” How clues are revealed

**Why:** Can't plant clues if you don't know solution.

### **The Fair Play Principle**

Reader should be able to solve it.

**Requirements:**
- All clues available to reader
- Detective doesn't withhold info
- Solution uses established facts
- No secret passages unknown to reader
- No undisclosed supernatural powers
- Culprit introduced early

**Golden Age Standard:**
Some readers want to solve it; play fair.

---

### **Red Herrings**

False leads that mislead.

**Purpose:**
- Increase difficulty
- Surprise reader
- Create suspense
- Make solution less obvious

**Balance:**
- Fair but misleading
- Explained later
- Don't overwhelm real clues
- Plausible at the time

**Example:**

## ğŸ¨ **Mystery Techniques**

### **The Unreliable Witness**

People lie or misremember.

**Why Unreliable:**
- Protecting themselves
- Protecting others
- Misunderstanding
- Poor memory
- Different perspective

**Use:** Creates false leads naturally.

---

### **The Missing Piece**

One crucial detail withheld.

**Technique:**
Reader has most clues but one key fact revealed at end.

**Caution:** Can feel like cheating if overdone.

---

### **Twist Suspects**

Subverting reader expectations.

**Classic Twists:**
- Least likely person
- First victim still alive
- Multiple murderers
- Narrator is killer
- No murder occurred
- Everyone did it

**Use Sparingly:** Readers know these now.

---

### **Ticking Clock**

Time pressure on investigation.

**Examples:**
- Killer will strike again
- Innocent person accused
- Evidence disappearing
- Political pressure
- Personal stakes

**Purpose:** Adds urgency to puzzle.

## âš ï¸ **Mystery Pitfalls**

### **1. Unfair Solution**

**Problem:** Clues withheld or solution impossible to deduce

**Solution:** Fair play principle; beta readers try to solve.

---

### **2. Obvious Culprit**

**Problem:** Too easy to guess

**Solution:** Multiple plausible suspects; misdirection.

---

### **3. Too Complex**

**Problem:** So many clues/suspects, reader lost

**Solution:** Clarity; limited suspect pool; timeline.

---

### **4. Deus Ex Machina**

**Problem:** Convenient coincidence solves case

**Solution:** Detective's work solves it, not luck.

---

### **5. Boring Detective**

**Problem:** Generic protagonist

**Solution:** Unique voice, quirks, personal stakes.
