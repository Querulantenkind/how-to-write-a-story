### **2. Fear vs. Terror vs. Horror**

**Ann Radcliffe's Distinction:**

**Terror:** 
- Anticipation of fear
- Dread of what might happen
- Psychological
- Often more effective

**Horror:**
- Reaction to fear
- Shock at what has happened
- Physical/visceral
- Impact moment

**Disgust:**
- Revulsion
- Physical response
- Visceral

**Best Horror:** Uses all three at different times.

## âš ï¸ **Horror Pitfalls**

### **1. Overexplaining**

**Problem:** Explaining away the mystery

**Solution:** Leave some ambiguity; unknown is scarier.

---

### **2. Gore for Gore's Sake**

**Problem:** Violence without purpose

**Solution:** Make violence meaningful; build to it.

### **3. Cheap Jump Scares**

**Problem:** Cat jumping out = lazy

**Solution:** Earn your scares through buildup.

### **4. Ending Without Impact**

**Problem:** Anticlimax or too neat

**Solution:** Endings should satisfy but unsettle.

---

### **5. Ignoring Consequences**

**Problem:** Horror without psychological impact

**Solution:** Show trauma, lasting effects.
