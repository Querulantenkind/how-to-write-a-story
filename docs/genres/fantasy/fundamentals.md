## âœï¸ **Writing Fantasy**

### **Start With the Rules**

**Before Writing:**
- Define magic system basics
- Establish world's history
- Map geography (roughly)
- Understand cultures
- Know what makes your world unique

**But Don't Over-Prepare:**
- You'll discover in drafting
- Don't get stuck in perpetual planning
- World-building is procrastination if you never write

---

### **Show, Don't Info-Dump**

**Avoid:**
```
"Welcome to Avaloria," the innkeeper said. "As you know, 
our kingdom was founded 500 years ago when the Dragon Wars 
ended and King Aldric unified the seven tribes..."
```

**Instead:**
```
The innkeeper pointed to the faded tapestry. "Dragon War 
victory, that is. My great-great-grandfather fought."

She nodded, having seen similar tapestries in every 
tavern from here to the capital. Five centuries later, 
they still celebrated killing the last dragon.
```

**Principle:** Reveal world through character experience, not exposition.

---

**Balance:**
- Exotic world â‰  alien emotions

**Ground the Strange:**

### **Establish Stakes Early**

## âš ï¸ **Common Fantasy Pitfalls**

### **1. Generic Medieval Europe**

**Problem:** Every fantasy = vaguely medieval England

**Solutions:**
- Draw from other cultures/time periods
- Create truly original cultures
- Question assumptions (why medieval? why monarchy?)
- Mix unexpected influences

---

### **2. Overpowered Characters**

**Problem:** Hero solves everything with magic

**Solutions:**
- Magic has costs
- Limited magical ability
- Non-magical problems
- Emotional/moral challenges

---

### **3. Info-Dumping**

**Problem:** Pages of exposition about world history

**Solutions:**
- Reveal through action
- Characters discover alongside reader
- Only include relevant information
- Trust reader to infer

---

### **4. Chosen One Without Earning It**

**Problem:** Hero special because prophecy says so

**Solutions:**
- Show character growth
- Earn the destiny
- Subvert the chosen one trope
- Make it about choice, not fate

---

### **5. Forgetting Consequences**

**Problem:** No costs to magic or violence

**Solutions:**
- Magic drains/costs
- Violence has psychological impact
- Decisions have lasting effects
- Actions matter
