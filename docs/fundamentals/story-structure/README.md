## ğŸ› ï¸ **How to Apply Structure**

### **During Planning:**

**1. Choose Your Primary Structure**
- Pick one that fits your story/genre
- Don't force ill-fitting structure

**2. Identify Key Moments**
- Mark major plot points
- Plan percentage/page placement

**3. Build Scenes Between Points**
- Use structure as skeleton
- Add flesh of actual scenes

---

### **During Drafting:**

**1. Use as Reference**
- Check when stuck
- Ensure forward momentum

**2. Don't Obsess**
- Let story breathe
- Discover as you write

**3. Trust the Process**
- Structure serves story
- Not vice versa

---

### **During Revision:**

**1. Diagnostic Tool**
- Map existing draft to structure
- Identify missing elements

**2. Problem-Solving**
- Pacing issues? Check act lengths
- Sagging middle? Check midpoint
- Weak ending? Check resolution

**3. Strengthen Weak Points**
- Add missing beats
- Emphasize key moments
- Reorder if necessary

## ğŸ“Š **Structure Myths Debunked**

### **Myth 1: "Structure kills creativity"**
**Truth:** Structure frees creativity by solving common problems, allowing you to focus on unique elements.

### **Myth 2: "All good stories follow the same structure"**
**Truth:** Many structures exist; different stories need different frameworks.

### **Myth 3: "Literary fiction doesn't need structure"**
**Truth:** Literary fiction has structureâ€”it's often just subtler or unconventional.

### **Myth 4: "Following structure makes your story formulaic"**
**Truth:** Structure is the skeleton, not the soul. Your characters, voice, and themes make it unique.

### **Myth 5: "You must pick one structure and stick to it"**
**Truth:** Many writers blend structures or use different ones for different aspects.
