> *Structure your mystery with clues, red herrings, and a satisfying revelation*

Mystery stories require careful planning to plant clues fairly while maintaining suspense. This template helps you create a complex mystery that plays fair with readers while keeping them guessing until the end.

## üéØ **Understanding Mystery Structure**

<details>
<summary><strong>The Mystery Promise</strong></summary>

### **Reader Expectations:**
- **A crime or puzzle is presented**
- **An investigator pursues the truth**
- **Clues are planted fairly**
- **Red herrings mislead but don't cheat**
- **The solution is surprising yet logical**
- **Justice or resolution is achieved**

### **Fair Play Rules:**
- ‚úÖ All clues must be visible to the reader
- ‚úÖ Detective can't have secret information
- ‚úÖ Solution must be logical from planted evidence
- ‚úÖ No deus ex machina solutions
- ‚úÖ The culprit must be introduced early
- ‚ùå No hidden magical powers
- ‚ùå No crucial evidence withheld from reader

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>How to Use This Template</strong></summary>

### **Planning Backward:**
1. **Start with the solution** ‚Äî Know whodunit and why
2. **Plant the clues** ‚Äî What evidence proves it?
3. **Add red herrings** ‚Äî What misleads?
4. **Map the investigation** ‚Äî How is truth discovered?
5. **Build suspense** ‚Äî Escalate danger and urgency

### **Tips:**
- ‚úÖ Know the full truth before writing
- ‚úÖ Plant clues throughout
- ‚úÖ Give suspects motive, means, opportunity
- ‚úÖ Multiple suspects with secrets
- ‚úÖ Fair play with readers
- ‚ùå Don't withhold key information
- ‚ùå Don't make culprit too obvious

</details>

## ‚úÖ **Mystery Requirements Checklist**

Ensure fair play:

### **The Crime:**
- [ ] Crime is clearly established
- [ ] Stakes are significant
- [ ] Time frame is clear
- [ ] Setting is vivid

### **The Investigation:**
- [ ] Detective has clear motivation
- [ ] Process is logical
- [ ] Obstacles are believable
- [ ] Pacing builds tension

### **The Clues:**
- [ ] All crucial clues are shown to reader
- [ ] Clues are distributed throughout
- [ ] Reader has same information as detective
- [ ] Red herrings play fair
- [ ] Solution is logical from evidence

### **The Suspects:**
- [ ] Multiple viable suspects
- [ ] Each has motive, means, opportunity
- [ ] Each has secrets to hide
- [ ] Culprit introduced early
- [ ] Others have believable innocence

### **The Solution:**
- [ ] Surprise but logical
- [ ] All clues make sense in retrospect
- [ ] No deus ex machina
- [ ] No withheld crucial information
- [ ] Satisfying reveal
- [ ] Justice achieved
