**Time Investment:** 1-3 hours


**Soft Magic** (1-3): Mysterious, rarely explained, creates wonder
- Example: Gandalf's magic in *Lord of the Rings*
- Best for: Atmosphere, wonder, threat

**Hard Magic** (7-10): Clear rules, defined limits, logical system
- Example: Allomancy in *Mistborn*
- Best for: Problem-solving, clever protagonists

**Middle Ground** (4-6): Some rules clear, some mystery remains
- Example: Magic in *Harry Potter*
- Best for: Flexibility with some consistency

**Why This Matters:**
- Soft magic should NOT solve major problems (feels like cheating)
- Hard magic CAN solve problems (reader understands and accepts)
- Match your hardness to how you'll use magic in plot

## ‚úÖ Consistency Checklist

### Before Drafting

- [ ] Core capabilities defined
- [ ] Hard limits established
- [ ] Costs specified
- [ ] Source explained (at least to yourself)
- [ ] Users defined
- [ ] Basic mechanics clear

### During Drafting

- [ ] Track every use of system
- [ ] Verify costs are paid
- [ ] Check against established limits
- [ ] Note any rules established in-text

### During Revision

- [ ] Audit for consistency
- [ ] Verify no "free" magic solves problems
- [ ] Confirm costs are shown
- [ ] Check that limits are maintained
- [ ] Ensure revelations are clear

---

## üí° Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Problem | Solution |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **Unlimited power** | No tension | Add meaningful costs and limits |
| **Inconsistent rules** | Reader distrust | Track and verify every use |
| **Magic solves everything** | No stakes | Ensure problems need more than magic |
| **Costs disappear** | Feels cheap | Show costs consistently |
| **Overly complex** | Confusing | Simplify or reveal gradually |
| **No impact on world** | Feels fake | Think through societal effects |
| **Power creep** | Escalation spiral | Stick to established limits |
