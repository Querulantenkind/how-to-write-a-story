## üéØ **Antagonist Design Principles**

<details>
<summary><strong>What Makes a Great Antagonist?</strong></summary>

### **Key Qualities:**
- **They believe they're right** ‚Äî From their perspective, they're the hero
- **They're formidable** ‚Äî They pose a genuine threat
- **They're specific** ‚Äî They have a personal stake, not generic evil
- **They're active** ‚Äî They pursue their own goals, not just react
- **They mirror the hero** ‚Äî They represent what the hero could become

### **Common Types:**
- **Classic Villain** ‚Äî Directly opposes hero with opposing goals
- **Sympathetic Antagonist** ‚Äî Understandable motives, wrong methods
- **Internal Antagonist** ‚Äî Hero's own flaws/fears
- **Systemic Antagonist** ‚Äî Institution, society, or nature
- **Reluctant Enemy** ‚Äî Forced into opposition by circumstances

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>How to Use This Template</strong></summary>

### **Process:**
1. **Start with their goal** ‚Äî What do they want?
2. **Establish their worldview** ‚Äî Why is it justified to them?
3. **Create their backstory** ‚Äî What made them this way?
4. **Design their methods** ‚Äî How far will they go?
5. **Mirror the protagonist** ‚Äî What do they represent?

### **Tips:**
- ‚úÖ Make them as complex as your protagonist
- ‚úÖ Give them a logical reason to oppose the hero
- ‚úÖ Show their humanity, even if they're cruel
- ‚úÖ Escalate their methods as stakes rise
- ‚ùå Avoid "evil for evil's sake"
- ‚ùå Don't make them incompetent

</details>
